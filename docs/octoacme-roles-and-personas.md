# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **With QA Lead**: Collaborate on test strategies, accept test findings, discuss coverage gaps
- **With UX Designer**: Implement design specs, provide technical feasibility feedback
- **With Product Manager**: Discuss requirements, estimate efforts, raise technical constraints
- **With Project Manager**: Track progress, escalate blockers, participate in planning

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **With UX Designer**: Define user research needs, review design concepts, validate against user goals
- **With Business Analyst**: Collaborate on requirements gathering, clarify business rules
- **With Project Manager**: Align on timelines, scope trade-offs, prioritization decisions
- **With Developers**: Discuss feasibility, estimate complexity, adjust scope

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **With Agile Coach**: Collaborate on process improvements, remove impediments, coach team on practices
- **With Business Analyst**: Ensure requirements are clear, identify scope gaps early
- **With All Roles**: Facilitate communications, track dependencies, escalate risks

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads own the quality assurance strategy, define test approaches, and ensure products meet quality standards and acceptance criteria before release. They work closely with developers, product managers, and operations to maintain test coverage and prevent production issues.

### Responsibilities
- Define comprehensive test strategies aligned with acceptance criteria
- Establish quality gates and success metrics for releases
- Design and maintain automated and manual test plans
- Collaborate with developers on test coverage and technical testing approaches
- Review quality metrics and identify improvement opportunities
- Coordinate testing activities across multiple teams when applicable
- Triage and manage defect backlogs
- Validate fixes and conduct release verification testing

### Goals
- Ensure product quality and reliability before and after release
- Reduce defects that reach production
- Establish and maintain high standards for test coverage
- Enable fast, confidence-driven releases

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups with development team
- Test status reports and defect summaries in sprint reviews
- Quality metrics and release readiness assessments
- Design and code review participation for testability

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **With Developers**: Collaborate on test automation, discuss technical testing approaches, validate fixes
- **With Product Manager**: Clarify acceptance criteria, align on quality standards and release readiness
- **With UX Designer**: Validate user experience flows, test usability scenarios
- **With Project Manager**: Report quality metrics, escalate blockers, assess release readiness
- **With Agile Coach**: Drive continuous improvement in testing practices

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers drive user-centered design practices and create design artifacts that guide product development. They conduct user research, create wireframes and prototypes, and collaborate with product, development, and business teams to ensure usability and alignment with customer needs.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and create user personas and journey maps
- Design wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity mockups
- Define information architecture and interaction patterns
- Create and maintain design systems and component libraries
- Conduct usability testing and iterate based on feedback
- Provide detailed design specifications and handoff to developers
- Advocate for user needs and usability in decision-making
- Review implemented features for design fidelity and UX consistency

### Goals
- Create intuitive, delightful user experiences that drive adoption
- Reduce user friction and support learning
- Maintain design consistency across products
- Enable developers to implement designs accurately and efficiently

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and feedback sessions with stakeholders
- Design specification documents and design system documentation
- Usability test findings and recommendations
- Collaboration in planning and backlog refinement meetings

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **With Product Manager**: Align on user research insights, validate design against business goals
- **With Developers**: Present design specifications, discuss technical feasibility, review implementation
- **With QA Lead**: Define usability test scenarios, validate UI/UX in testing
- **With Agile Coach**: Establish design review cadence, improve design collaboration practices
- **With Business Analyst**: Understand user requirements, validate business rules in design

---

## Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Agile Coaches support teams in adopting and improving agile practices, facilitating ceremonies, removing impediments, and driving continuous improvement in team collaboration and delivery predictability.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate agile ceremonies (standups, planning, retrospectives, reviews)
- Coach team members on agile principles and best practices
- Identify and escalate team impediments and blockers
- Drive retrospective discussions and track action items
- Support team health assessments and psychological safety
- Mentor project managers and leads on agile principles
- Establish and refine team working agreements
- Track and report on team velocity, burndown, and delivery metrics

### Goals
- Enable high-performing, self-organizing teams
- Improve delivery predictability and cycle time
- Foster psychological safety and continuous improvement culture
- Reduce unnecessary dependencies and blockers

### Typical Communication
- Facilitation of daily standups, planning, retro meetings
- Weekly metrics dashboards and trend analysis
- Coaching conversations with team members and leads
- Recommendations for process improvements

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **With Project Manager**: Partner on impediment removal, refine planning and tracking practices
- **With All Roles**: Facilitate collaboration, coach on agile practices, support team health
- **With QA Lead and Developers**: Coach on testing practices and quality metrics
- **With Product Manager**: Support backlog refinement, help align on sprint scope

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge product, business, and technical teams by gathering, clarifying, and documenting requirements. They ensure that business needs are clearly understood, that acceptance criteria are well-defined, and that solutions deliver against business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct requirements discovery and stakeholder interviews
- Document business requirements and use cases
- Create detailed acceptance criteria and definition of done specifications
- Clarify scope and identify gaps early in the project lifecycle
- Validate business rules and processes
- Support product managers in backlog refinement and prioritization
- Facilitate communication between business stakeholders and technical teams
- Conduct solution validation and acceptance testing against business requirements

### Goals
- Reduce rework and scope creep through clear requirements documentation
- Accelerate development by eliminating ambiguity
- Ensure delivered solutions meet business objectives
- Improve team collaboration between business and technical functions

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and specification reviews
- Backlog refinement sessions and acceptance criteria workshops
- Stakeholder interviews and findings summaries
- Acceptance testing reports and validation findings

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **With Product Manager**: Collaborate on requirements gathering, define acceptance criteria, validate prioritization
- **With Developers**: Clarify requirements, discuss technical feasibility, support implementation questions
- **With QA Lead**: Define acceptance test scenarios, validate business requirements
- **With Project Manager**: Identify scope and requirement gaps early, support risk identification
- **With Business Stakeholders**: Gather requirements, validate understanding, conduct acceptance validation

## How these personas are used in the exercise

- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- Recognize that team members may wear multiple personas; use these definitions to clarify responsibilities and expected interactions regardless of organizational structure.
- Leverage interaction matrices to understand handoff points and dependencies between roles across project lifecycle phases (Planning, Execution, QA, Release).

## Cross-Role Interaction Matrix

This matrix shows key touchpoints and dependencies between roles throughout the project lifecycle:

| Phase | Primary Owner | Key Collaborators | Handoff/Dependency |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Initiation** | Product Manager | Project Manager, Business Analyst, Stakeholders | Requirements → Planning |
| **Planning** | Project Manager | Product Manager, Business Analyst, All Leads | Scope → Execution |
| **Design** | UX Designer | Product Manager, Developers, Business Analyst | Design Specs → Development |
| **Development** | Developers | QA Lead, UX Designer, Agile Coach | Code → Testing |
| **Testing** | QA Lead | Developers, UX Designer, Product Manager | Test Results → Release |
| **Release** | Project Manager | QA Lead, Developers, Product Manager | Verified Build → Production |
| **Retrospective** | Agile Coach | All Roles, Project Manager | Learnings → Continuous Improvement |
